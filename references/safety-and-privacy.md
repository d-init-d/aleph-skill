# Safety and privacy

This skill is for lawful, evidence-backed simulation. It is not a tool for surveillance, doxxing, private profiling, or access-control bypass.

## Allowed

- Public historical analysis.
- Public-role behavior modeling for officials, executives, authors, researchers, or other public figures when relevant to a simulation.
- Public institutional and organizational behavior modeling.
- Open-source evidence ledgers.
- Counterfactual and scenario analysis with uncertainty labels.

## Refuse or narrow

Refuse or narrow requests that involve:

- minors,
- private individuals outside public-role context,
- stalking, harassment, doxxing, or intimidation,
- home addresses, private contact details, family/private-life details,
- private social accounts or leaked data,
- medical, financial, legal, or protected-trait speculation,
- real-time location or whereabouts,
- login, paywall, captcha, rate-limit, or anti-bot bypass,
- stolen credentials or cookies,
- instructions to manipulate real people.

A local safety phrase such as “do not collect” or “never reveal” negates only the harmful term in that clause. It does not sanitize a later request joined with “but”, “however”, or another contrasting clause. Evaluate every harmful match in its own local context and refuse the whole mixed request when any requested action remains disallowed.

## Living people

For living people, keep dossiers public-role-only and simulation-relevant. Use phrases such as “public record suggests” and “the model assumes” rather than claiming private motives.

Frozen knowledge packets and roleplay outputs use closed schemas. Reject unknown fields, post-cutoff claims, absent access bases, private contact or sensitive-personal content, hidden probability fields, and private-motive text at any nesting depth. Recomputing a packet hash never makes disallowed content valid.

## Emotional and psychological claims

Emotional factors may be modeled only when supported by public evidence or as explicit assumptions. Do not diagnose or invent inner states. Prefer observable behavior patterns and institutional incentives.

## Access limits

When a source is blocked:

1. record the blocked source,
2. try lawful public alternatives,
3. report what the user could retrieve manually,
4. do not bypass.

## Output labels

Use clear labels:

- `fact`: directly sourced.
- `inference`: reasoned from evidence.
- `assumption`: user-provided or model-stated premise.
- `simulation`: generated branch or effect.
- `counterfactual`: known deviation from observed history.
